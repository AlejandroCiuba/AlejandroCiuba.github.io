About Me

I am a current undergraduate student at The University of Pittsburgh where I am in my 3rd year of pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science with minors in Spanish and Linguistics. I am originally from Hawley, Pennsylvania, but I relocate to Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania during the school year. For current professional achievements: I am doing linguistics research under Dr. Alan Juffs and Dr. Na-Rae Han, I am a teaching assistant to Dr. John Ramirez for CS445, and I serve as a Provost Academy Leader. Regarding past achievements: I won CSC Hacks 2020, the school's annual hackathon, I voluteered at the Carnegie Library's ESL Class, and I have served on numerous academic councils. My main interests in research are video-game design and natural-language processing. Finally, my hobbies are: video game delevopment, writing short stories, and Spanish learning.
